# Made to Stick – Core Framework (SUCCESs)

**Sticky = Understandable + Memorable + Changes thought or behavior.**

**The enemy**: Curse of Knowledge — we forget what it’s like not to know.

**The solution**: SUCCESs checklist (apply to every section):

## 1. Simple
- Find the **core** (Commander’s Intent — one sentence).
- Strip to the essence; use proverbs, schemas, or high-concept analogies.
- Compact: if you can’t say it on a Post-it, it’s not simple enough.

## 2. Unexpected
- Break the guessing machine.
- Use surprise or a knowledge gap.
- Postdictability test: after the reveal, it feels inevitable.

## 3. Concrete
- Human-scale, sensory, specific.
- Velcro memory: examples that stick in the mind.
- Avoid abstract numbers without a human-scale translation (years of siege, days of march, weight of tribute).

## 4. Credible
- Named sources, vivid details, testable claims.
- Primary voices and dated maps beat vague authority.
- Anti-authority or hard limits (what archaeology cannot show) can increase credibility.

## 5. Emotional
- One vivid person > abstract masses.
- Identity and belonging matter in historical memory — handle carefully and honestly.
- Let stakes appear through scenes, not sermonizing.

## 6. Stories
- Use **Challenge**, **Connection**, or **Creativity** plots when they fit real events.
- Stories act as flight simulators — readers simulate and remember.
- Spot the moment someone faced a real obstacle with real constraints.

**Compact writing pattern for a historical beat**:
1. Core message (Simple)  
2. Unexpected hook or gap  
3. Concrete human-scale scene  
4. Credible evidence (tied to the lesson pack)  
5. Emotional payoff (why this still matters in the mind, not a sales pitch)  
6. Story that carries the memory  

**Final stress test**:
- Would an intelligent reader with no prior expertise understand it, remember it, and retell it?
- Does it stay faithful to the lesson pack’s facts and honesty labels?
- Would it change how they picture this piece of the past?

(Extracted and adapted from *Made to Stick* by Chip Heath & Dan Heath, 2007/2008.)
