# On Writing Well – Core Principles (William Zinsser)

**Core Idea**: Good nonfiction writing is clear thinking made visible. The secret is to strip away clutter and let the writer’s humanity show.

**Checklist (apply to every paragraph and the whole piece)**:
- **Clarity**: The first requirement. Make every sentence say exactly what you mean. Avoid ambiguity.
- **Simplicity**: Use plain words, short sentences. Prefer the familiar word to the far-fetched.
- **Clutter**: Ruthlessly eliminate every unnecessary word, phrase, jargon, adverb, and adjective.
- **Humanity / Warmth**: Be yourself. Let personality and warmth show. Use active voice and concrete details.
- **Unity**: Every piece should have a single theme or point. Everything supports it.
- **Strong Lead and Ending**: Hook the reader immediately; end with a sense of resolution or forward-looking insight.
- **Rewriting**: Writing is rewriting. Read your work aloud. Cut what doesn’t work.

**History tip**: Unity means one Core Message for the whole narrative — not a tour of every primary question in the lesson ledger.
